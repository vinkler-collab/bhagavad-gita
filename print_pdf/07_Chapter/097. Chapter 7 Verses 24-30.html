Chapter 7 — vijñāna yoga

097. Chapter 7 Verses 24-30

Lectures by Swami Sarvapriyananda
Verse 7.24
अव्यक्तं व्यक्तिमापन्नं मन्यन्ते मामबुद्धय: ।
परं भावमजानन्तो ममाव्ययमनुत्तमम् ।। 24।।
avyaktaṃ vyaktimāpannaṃ
manyante māmabuddhayaḥ |
paraṃ bhāvamajānanto
mamāvyayamanuttamam ||
◈ avyaktam – the unmanifest; vyaktim – manifestation/human form; āpannam – having come to;
◈ manyante – think; mām – Me; abuddhayaḥ – the unintelligent;
◈ param – supreme; bhāvam – state/nature; ajānantaḥ – not knowing;
◈ mama – My; avyayam – imperishable; anuttamam – unsurpassed.
Not knowing My supreme Nature, immutable and transcendent, foolish men think that I, the Unmanifest, am endowed with a manifest form.
Verse 7.25
नाहं प्रकाश: सर्वस्य योगमायासमावृत: ।
मूढोऽयं नाभिजानाति लोको मामजमव्ययम् ।। 25।।
nāhaṃ prakāśaḥ sarvasya
yogamāyāsamāvṛtaḥ |
mūḍho’yaṃ nābhijānāti
loko māmajamavyayam ||
◈ na – not; aham – I; prakāśaḥ – manifest/visible; sarvasya – to all;
◈ yoga-māyā-samāvṛtaḥ – veiled by Yoga-Maya;
◈ mūḍhaḥ – deluded; ayam – this; na abhijānāti – does not recognize;
◈ lokaḥ – world; mām – Me; ajam – unborn; avyayam – imperishable.
Veiled by My māyā born of the gunas, I am not revealed to all. This deluded world knows Me not as the unborn and eternal.
Verse 7.26
वेदाहं समतीतानि वर्तमानानि चार्जुन ।
भविष्याणि च भूतानि मां तु वेद न कश्चन ।। 26।।
vedāhaṃ samatītāni
vartamānāni cārjuna |
bhaviṣyāṇi ca bhūtāṇi
māṃ tu veda na kaścana ||
◈ veda – know; aham – I; samatītāni – the past;
◈ vartamānāni – the present; ca – and; arjuna – O Arjuna;
◈ bhaviṣyāṇi – the future; ca – and; bhūtāni – all beings;
◈ mām – Me; tu – but; veda – knows; na kaścana – no one.
O Arjuna! I know the past, present, and the future of all beings, but Me no one knows.
Verse 7.27
इच्छाद्वेषसमुत्थेन द्वन्द्वमोहेन भारत ।
सर्वभूतानि सम्मोहं सर्गे यान्ति परन्तप ।। 27।।
icchādveṣasamutthena
dvandvamohena bhārata |
sarvabhūtāni sammohaṃ
sarge yānti parantapa ||
◈ icchā – desire; dveṣa – aversion; samutthena – arising from;
◈ dvandva-mohena – by the delusion of pairs of opposites; bhārata – O descendant of Bharata;
◈ sarva-bhūtāni – all beings; sammoham – to delusion;
◈ sarge – in the world/at birth; yānti – go/fall; parantapa – O scorcher of foes.
O Arjuna! By the delusion of pairs of opposites caused by desire and aversion, all beings are subject to illusion in the world.
Verse 7.28
येषां त्वन्तगतं पापं जनानां पुण्यकर्मणाम् ।
ते द्वन्द्वमोहनिर्मुक्ता भजन्ते मां दृढव्रता: ।। 28।।
yeṣāṃ tvantagataṃ pāpaṃ
janānāṃ puṇyakarmaṇām |
te dvandvamohanirmuktā
bhajante māṃ dṛḍhavratāḥ ||
◈ yeṣām – of those; tu – but; antagatam – has come to an end; pāpam – sin;
◈ janānām – of men; puṇya-karmaṇām – of virtuous deeds;
◈ te – they; dvandva-moha-nirmuktāḥ – freed from the delusion of the pairs of opposites;
◈ bhajante – worship; mām – Me; dṛḍha-vratāḥ – with firm resolve.
But those men of good works whose sin has come to an end, worship Me steadfast in vows, freed from the delusive pairs of opposites.
Verse 7.29
जरामरणमोक्षाय मामाश्रित्य यतन्ति ये ।
ते ब्रह्म तद्विदु: कृत्स्नमध्यात्मं कर्म चाखिलम् ।। 29।।
jarāmaraṇamokṣāya
māmāśritya yatanti ye |
te brahma tadviduḥ kṛtsnam
adhyātmaṃ karma cākhilam ||
◈ jarā-maraṇa-mokṣāya – for liberation from old age and death;
◈ mām-āśritya – taking refuge in Me; yatanti – strive; ye – who;
◈ te – they; brahma – Brahman; tat – that; viduḥ – know; kṛtsnam – the whole;
◈ adhyātmam – the Self; karma – action; ca – and; akhilam – entire.
Those who take shelter in Me, striving for liberation from old-age and death, come to know the Brahman, the individual self, and the entire field of karmic action.
Verse 7.30
साधिभूताधिदैवं मां साधियज्ञं च ये विदु: ।
प्रयाणकालेऽपि च मां ते विदुर्युक्तचेतस: ।। 30।।
sādhibhūtādhidaivaṃ māṃ
sādhiyajñaṃ ca ye viduḥ |
prayāṇakāle’pi ca māṃ
te viduryuktacetasaḥ ||
◈ sa-adhibhūta-adhidaivam – with the Adhibhuta (elements) and the Adhidaiva (divine power); mām – Me;
◈ sa-adhiyajñam – with the Adhiyajna (the Lord of sacrifice); ca – and; ye – who; viduḥ – know;
◈ prayāṇa-kāle api – even at the time of death; ca – and; mām – Me;
◈ te – they; viduḥ – know; yukta-cetasaḥ – of steady mind.
Those who know Me as the One that underlies all the elements, as the One that underlies all the gods, and as the One that sustains all the sacrifices, will, with steadfast mind, know Me even in the hour of death.
